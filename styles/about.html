What is autistic masking?

Masking is a strategy used by some autistic people, consciously or unconsciously, to appear non-autistic. While this strategy can help them get by at school, work and in social situations, it can have a devastating impact on mental health, sense of self and access to an autism diagnosis.

Because masking is real and it’s rarely talked about in a way that captures the emotional side. It’s not just a behaviour; it’s a burden. This project is for anyone who wants to understand, connect, or reflect. It’s not about fixing anything. It’s about seeing what’s been hidden.

This website lets you step into that world not just read about it. Through a series of interactive videos and immersive scenarios, you’ll see what it feels like to navigate everyday spaces while masking. You’ll hear internal thoughts, feel the pressure to act “normal,” and sense the tension of hiding your true self. We didn’t build this to explain autism. We built it to make you feel something.
